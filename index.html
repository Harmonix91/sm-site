Leadership is the lever.

Capital can be raised. Assets can be bought. Leadership has to be built. Every value creation plan lives or dies in the hands of the people running the core functions. The right operator will compound performance year on year. The wrong one will quietly drain it.

01
Most hiring mistakes are avoidable.
They're made in the brief, not the search. The wrong hire is almost always visible on day one if the right questions got asked in week one.
02
Speed is a feature, not a KPI.
Long searches don't produce better hires. They produce tired boards and compromised shortlists. Every week past month four costs you optionality.
03
Generalist CVs don't run specialist assets.
The people who can actually run a battery platform, a solar IPP, or a fibre build-out are a small, specific pool. Most search firms don't know who they are.
04
Process is the product.
A retained, exclusive, structured search is the only honest model. Anything else is a sourcing team in a suit.
